Veg Biryani

About This Recipe

Veg Biryani is a fragrant medley of long-grain basmati rice, tender vegetables, and warming spices like cardamom, cloves, and saffron. Each layer is slow-cooked to perfection, creating a burst of flavor in every bite. Balanced with herbs and caramelized onions, this regal dish is a timeless vegetarian centerpiece often served with raita or salan.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups Basmati Rice (soaked 30 mins)
  • 1 cup Mixed Vegetables (carrots, beans, peas, cauliflower, potato)
  • 1 Onion (sliced)
  • 1 Tomato (chopped)
  • 1/4 cup Mint Leaves
  • 1/4 cup Coriander Leaves
  • 2 Green Chilies (slit)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1/2 cup Curd (yogurt)
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tsp Biryani Masala
  • 1 Bay Leaf
  • 4 Cloves
  • 1-inch Cinnamon Stick
  • 2 Cardamom Pods
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds
  • 1/4 tsp Saffron (soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk)
  • 3 tbsp Ghee/Oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Prep Rice: Parboil rice with 3 cups water, bay leaf, and salt until 70% cooked. Drain and set aside.
  2. Cook Veggies: Heat ghee/oil. Add whole spices (cumin, cloves, cinnamon, cardamom). Saut√© onions until golden.
  3. Spice Base: Add ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, tomatoes, and cook until soft. Stir in turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, and biryani masala.
  4. Layer 1: In a heavy-bottomed pot, spread half the rice. Top with cooked veggies, mint, coriander, and saffron milk.
  5. Layer 2: Add remaining rice. Drizzle 2 tbsp ghee and 1/4 cup water. Cover with a tight lid.
  6. Dum Cook: Cook on low heat for 20-25 mins. Let it rest for 10 mins before serving.
  7. Serve: Fluff gently with a fork. Pair with raita or mirchi ka salan.
